Abolfazl Doostparast Torshizi

My name is Abolfazl. I am a senior research scientist at Wang Genomics Lab at Perelman School of Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania. I am also a proud member of Raymond G. Perelman Center for Cellular and Molecular Therapeutics at the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, the birthplace of LUXTURNA®, the first FDA-approved gene therapy for Inherited Retinal Blindness as well as Kymria®, a CAR T-cell-based therapy for acute lymphoblastic leukemia.

Prior to moving to Philadelphia, we were a part of Department of Biomedical Informatics and Institute for Genomic Medicine at Columbia University. I did my postdoc in Computer Science and Systems Biology at the University of California, Santa Barbara and Harvard University. I earned my PhD in Industrial and Systems Engineering (minor in System biology) from Amirkabir University of Technology (former Tehran Polytechnic), Iran, in 2015. I am broadly interested in Computational Systems Biology of complex diseases, Network and Cell-Type Deconvolution, Single-Cell Genomics, and Bioinformatics.

I have been very fortunate to have established collaborations with a large group of scientists from a diverse set of institutions including: The University of Chicago, The University of California San Francisco (UCSF), University of Washington, Institute for Systems Biology (ISB), New York University, Ichan School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, Columbia University Medical Center, Jinan University (China), University of Denver, University of Southern California, and The University of Texas Health System.
